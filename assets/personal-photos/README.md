# Personal Photos for Home Page

## What to Add

You need **2 personal photos** for the home page quad grid layout:

---

### Photo 1: Headshot/Professional (Quadrant 2 — Top Right)

**Purpose:** Main profile photo next to your short bio

**Suggested filename:** `howard-headshot.jpg` or `profile.jpg`

**What works:**
- Professional headshot (face clearly visible)
- Semi-professional (smart casual, good lighting)
- Outdoor portrait (natural, approachable)
- Should show your face clearly — people connect with faces

**Technical specs:**
- Format: JPG or PNG
- Minimum size: 800x800px (will be cropped to circle or rounded square)
- File size: <2MB ideally
- Good lighting, sharp focus

---

### Photo 2: Candid/Working (Quadrant 4 — Bottom Right)

**Purpose:** Show you in action — building, working, creating

**Suggested filename:** `howard-working.jpg` or `howard-building.jpg` or `howard-action.jpg`

**What works:**
- Photo of you building/soldering/coding
- Working on a project
- At a desk/workspace
- Presenting/teaching
- Candid moment that shows personality
- Action shot (not posed)

**Alternative:** If you don't have a good candid photo, we can:
- Use a project collage (2-3 small project thumbnails)
- Design a timeline graphic in Figma
- Leave empty/use abstract visual

**Technical specs:**
- Format: JPG or PNG
- Minimum size: 1200x800px (landscape) or 800x1200px (portrait)
- File size: <3MB ideally
- Good composition, tells a story

---

## How to Add Photos

1. Place your photos in this folder: `assets/personal-photos/`
2. Name them descriptively (e.g., `howard-headshot.jpg`, `howard-working.jpg`)
3. Update `page-1-home.md` if you use different filenames
4. Commit and push to GitHub

---

## Design Usage

**Quadrant 2 (Top Right):**
- Your headshot will be cropped to a circle or rounded square (200-300px)
- Placed above or beside your name and short bio
- Clean, professional presentation

**Quadrant 4 (Bottom Right):**
- Candid photo displayed full-bleed or with padding
- Complements the text-heavy Quadrant 3
- Adds personality and visual interest
- Should feel authentic, not overly polished

---

## Tips for Good Photos

**Headshot:**
- Face the camera, natural smile or neutral expression
- Good lighting (natural light from window is great)
- Solid background or blurred background
- Dress how you want to be perceived (professional but approachable)

**Candid/Working:**
- Natural lighting is best
- Show your workspace, tools, projects
- Don't pose — capture a real moment
- It's okay if it's not perfect — authenticity > polish

---

## Need Help?

If you don't have suitable photos:
- Take new ones with your phone (iPhone cameras are excellent)
- Ask a friend to take some candid shots while you work
- Use existing photos from LinkedIn, social media, etc.
- We can work with what you have — no need for professional photography
