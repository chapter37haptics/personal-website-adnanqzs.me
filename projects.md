## Projects

#### Age 15 (2010)

**Monster Truck LED Add-ons**
Added 3 LEDs in parallel to RC monster truck to have more fun at night! Velcro-mounted battery for easy replacement after high-impact crashes.

#### Age 16 (2011)

**LED Beats — Woofer Version**
Made listening to music more exciting by making LEDs glow according to the beats of music. Had to "jail break" into to subwoofer and connect 9 LEDs. Later upgraded with 28 white LEDs salvaged from broken emergency light. Turned room into a "mini disco."

**Arc Reactor Model**
Inspired by Iron Man. Salvaged parts from old VCR player. Installed alternating blue and white LEDs.

**Photo Frame Gift**
Handmade birthday gift: cardboard frame with blue and white LEDs. Magnetic attachment system made the frame modular and allowed photo swaps.

**Portable Fan**
Problem: 2-hour power cuts at school. Solution: Extracted motor from CD-ROM, added 4-battery holder and clip. Later improved with better base and magnetic mounting.

**Playstation charger repair**
The plug's pin broke and I didn't want to spend money on a new charger so I opened it up, soldered a new plug to the AC terminals of the charger and glued everything back together. Looked ugly but worked just fine!

#### Age 17 (2012)

**Magentic Portable Fan**
Improved portable fan with magnetic mounting and adjustable angle.

**Infrared Camera Conversion**
Hacked old camera into infrared camera by removing infrared filter and adding photo film to block visible light. Could almost see the heat profile emitted from an oven & a bulb.

**Earphone Repair**
Learned to replace 3.5mm audio jacks using a soldering iron. Biggest win: salvaged friend's broken Beats earphones ($99 retail) and repaired them for under $0.50.

**LED Beats — Portable Version**
Made the woofer LED project compact & modular. It was a prototype for a bigger goal: connect any LED strip to any music system!

**Solar Panel Cell Phone Charger**
Built "green" charger with a solar panel, 5 Volt regulator circuit, and phone charging pin. While the phone charged, it took too long. This is when I understood why solar power is still not practical LOL.

**Portable USB Charger**
Frequent traveler solution: 5V regulator + female USB port + 9V battery. Casing made from repurposed food container. Charging time was longer but it was a good backup during emergencies.

**555 Timer IC Circuit**
This was my first experience with integrated circuits (ICs). Made LEDs blink with capacitors, resistors, and ICs. Learnt a lot by burning a couple of these cheap ICs.

#### Age 18 (2013)

**First Robot**
Built line follower and obstacle avoidance robots using L293D drivers, infrared sensors, and motors. Learned C programming, PIC microcontrollers (MPLABS), and wireless control (XBEE). Completed 40-hour course in 20 hours. Started self-learning Arduino via YouTube.

**Photo stand**
Made a custom photo stand with adjustable brightness (using a variable resistor) for the lights. Improved on the finishing compared to the photo frame made at age 16

**Electronic dice**
Used an arduino and a random number generator to light up a random number of LEDs, essentially mimicing a dice. Made this just to kill time during freshmen year.

#### Age 19 (2014)

**Laser Shooting Game**
Made a fun little shooting game which drops a target if the laser hits the center! I was really bored one night during an internship and wanted a fun way to learn & control servo motors with Arduino.

**Automatic Night Light - Birthday gift**
Made a lamp that lights up automatically in the dark. Used a light dependant resistor and 555 IC to trigger and control the LEDs. By now I had become a pro at using a soldering iron and drawing circuit diagrams!

**Wireless Energy Circuit**
I saw a YouTube video about wireless energy transfer via mutual induction to light a bulb so had to try it! It kinda used the transformer concept of AC power but with DC power which was fascinating!

#### Age 20 (2015)

**Push button game**
As VP of EE club, my goal was to attract students to sign up as members so I created a game by staying up all night using an Arduino, LCD screen, LEDs and a push button. It created a crazy amount of buzz and we saw a record number of signups!

#### Age 21 (2016)
**LED Workshop**
I created an LED workshop to give a chance to students from all majors a taste of building circuits in a fun way. They learnt how to build two circuits - Blinking LEDs, LEDs glow to beats of music.

#### Age 22 (2017)

**Portable Air Cooler**
I visited a friend during summer in Mumbai and it was HOT AS HELL. Her landlord doesn't provide her with an AC so I made a portable one! Throw in some ice and cool air comes your way.
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_CIVUyPJsHI

**AI Drone to Inspect Power Lines**
This was my senior year thesis in undergrad. My friend and I built an drone which streamed live video to a remote server and recorded coordinates of power lines with faulty insulators. This was when I knew I wanted to study AI/ML and started filling applications for grad school.

#### Age 23 (2018)

**GoPro Auto-Panorama Gadget**
I loved taking timelapses on my GoPro but always wanted it to also take a panoramic timelapse so I built a gadget to turn the GoPro automatically!
