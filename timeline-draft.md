# Howard Stark — Timeline of Building & Achievements

## Early Years: The Foundation (2012-2013)

### Age 15 (2012)
**Monster Truck LED Add-ons**
- First documented project: Added 3 LEDs in parallel to RC monster truck with battery holder and switch
- Velcro-mounted battery for easy replacement after high-impact crashes
- *Learning: Practical application of circuits, dealing with mechanical stress*

### Age 16 (2013)

**Portable Fan — Version 1**
- Problem: 2-hour power cuts at school, sweating in the heat
- Solution: Extracted CD-ROM motor (1.5V), 4-battery holder, clip attachment
- Iteration: Improved with better base and magnetic mounting for versatility

**Photo Frame Gift**
- Out-of-the-box birthday gift for a friend
- Handmade cardboard frame with blue and white LEDs wired in series
- Magnetic attachment system allowing photo swaps
- *Learning: Creating functional gifts, thinking about user experience*

**Arc Reactor Model (Iron Man)**
- Inspired by Iron Man movie — wanted to emulate Tony Stark
- Salvaged parts from old VCR player mom was throwing away
- Installed alternating blue and white LEDs
- Too heavy to mount on chest with a belt
- *Note: Early sign of builder mindset + resourcefulness*

**AIESEC Recruitment**
- Minimum age requirement: 18 years old
- Howard: 16 years old
- VP of AIESEC Hyderabad initially refused due to age
- Showed portfolio of projects above
- **Result:** Recruited despite being 2 years under minimum age
- Awarded a kurta at first group meet (out of 30 people) for "most outstanding AIESEC'er"
- *Learning: Work speaks louder than age*

**LED Beats — Woofer Version**
- Idea: Make listening to music more exciting
- Opened subwoofer, connected 9 LEDs in series with resistance to woofer
- LEDs pulse with the beat of the music
- Later iteration: Salvaged 28 white LEDs from broken emergency light, connected in parallel
- Mounted on curtain rod → turned room into "mini disco"
- *Learning: Applied series/parallel resistance grouping in real use case*

### Age 17 (2014)

**Photo Stand Gift**
- Another handmade birthday gift
- PVC board structure with 4 LEDs at vertices
- Added 10k potentiometer for variable brightness control
- Battery holder attached with Velcro for easy replacement
- *Learning: Iteration on gift-making, added dimming feature*

**Portable USB Charger**
- Problem: Frequent traveler, needed portable charging
- Solution: 5V regulator, female USB port, 9V battery
- Casing: Repurposed food container (best out of waste)
- Ripped USB cable from old mouse, connected to Nokia charging pin
- Compact and handy for emergencies

**Solar Panel Cell Phone Charger**
- "Go Green" motivation
- Purchased 6V 8W solar panel
- Built 5V regulator circuit with transistor and capacitors
- Connected to cell phone charging pin
- *Learning: Sustainability + electronics*

**LED Beats — Portable Version**
- Goal: Make the woofer LED project compact and portable
- Used TIP 31 transistor, wired with 2 LEDs, 3V button cell, 3.5mm audio jack
- Prototype for larger project: "Setting up a whole room with beat-reactive lights"

**Earphone Repair Mastery**
- Problem: Buying new earphones every 6 months = waste of money
- Root cause: Wear and tear near 3.5mm audio jack
- Learned to replace audio jack by studying old earphones + internet research
- **Big win:** Friend threw away broken Beats earphones (original, $99 retail)
- Replaced cable section with old AUX cable
- Cost: Less than $0.50
- Minor trade-off: Slightly reduced volume due to solder resistance
- *Learning: Repair > replace; value creation through salvage*

**PSP Charger Repair**
- Sat on PSP charger, broke one AC pin
- Opened casing, replaced pins with plug from old appliance
- Soldered to board → working again
- Saved: $10

**Infrared Camera Conversion**
- Converted old camera to infrared camera
- Removed IR-filtering film from camera sensor (blocks infrared radiation)
- Added piece of old photo film to block visible light spectrum
- Use case: Sneak around in the dark with infrared LED, no lights needed
- *Learning: Hardware hacking, understanding light spectrum*

**555 Timer IC Circuit**
- Found blinking LED project online using capacitors, resistors, and ICs
- First attempt: Failed
- Tried again days later: Success
- Didn't fully understand how it worked, but knew one thing: "I had advanced to the next level"
- *Learning: Persistence pays off; complexity is a ladder to climb*
